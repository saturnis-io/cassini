## Understanding the AGPL-3.0

The Community Edition of Cassini is licensed under the **GNU Affero General Public License v3.0 (AGPL-3.0)**, a strong copyleft license maintained by the Free Software Foundation.

Here is what that means in practice:

- **You can freely use, modify, and distribute Cassini** under the terms of the AGPL-3.0. There is no restriction on commercial use.
- **The key requirement:** If you modify Cassini and make it available to users over a network (including internal company networks, intranets, or as a SaaS offering), the AGPL requires you to make your complete modified source code available to all users of that service.
- **Unmodified use is straightforward.** If you deploy Cassini without modifications, the AGPL obligations are minimal. The source code is already public.
- **This is what makes open source sustainable.** Improvements flow back to the community, and everyone benefits from a stronger platform.

The full license text is in the [LICENSE](LICENSE) file at the root of this repository.

## FAQ

**Can I use the Community Edition for my business?**

Absolutely. The Community Edition is full-featured SPC software. You can run it in production, use it for real quality control, and rely on it for your manufacturing processes. Just follow the AGPL-3.0 terms.

**What happens when my commercial license expires?**

Commercial features enter read-only mode. Your data is never locked and can always be exported. The Community Edition features continue working normally. You will not lose access to your data.

**Can I try commercial features before purchasing?**

Yes. Contact [sales@saturnis.io](mailto:sales@saturnis.io) for a time-limited trial license.

**Is the license per-server or per-organization?**

Per organization. Deploy Cassini on as many servers, containers, and environments as you need within your organization. No per-seat or per-instance charges.

**Can I switch from AGPL to a commercial license later?**

Yes. You can start with the Community Edition and upgrade to a commercial license at any time. Your deployment and data carry over seamlessly.

**Do I need a commercial license for internal tools?**

If you are running Cassini without modifications, the AGPL is fine for internal use. If you are making modifications and providing the service to users within your organization over a network, the AGPL requires source availability to those users. A commercial license removes that obligation.
